2 kilometers north of Hikkaduwa, on the beach, is the Turtle Hatchery. The hatchery is a tiny, private research facility that demonstrates the turtles' progressive growth and works to preserve marine turtles, which are in risk of extinction. For an additional price, there are some locations where you can also get the chance to release newborn turtles into the huge ocean. March through April are the greatest months to go and watch the turtles.

Things to do ......

1. Observe Turtle Conservation Activities:
Visitors can observe how baby turtles are hatched and then released into the ocean. This is a truly unique experience, and visitors can also witness the feeding of adult turtles with seaweed.

2. Learn About Different Species of Turtles:
The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery is home to five species of sea turtles, including green turtles, hawksbill turtles, and loggerhead turtles. Visitors can learn about the different species of turtles and their behavior.

3. Visit the Aquarium:
Visitors can see baby turtles, sea snails, and other marine life in the aquarium. This allows visitors to see the turtle conservation efforts up close.

4. Support Conservation Efforts:
Visitors can contribute by sponsoring a turtle hatchery or adopting a sea turtle. This supports the conservation efforts and ensures the safety of the turtle population.
